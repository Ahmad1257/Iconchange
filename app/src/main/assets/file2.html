Q#:2.2. A train starts from rest. It moves through 1km in 100s with uniform acceleration. What will be its speed at the end of 100s?

Ans. Solution:

Given data:

                     Vi = 0 ms-1

                     S = 1 k m = 1000 m

                      t = 100 s 

To Find:

                     Vf = ?

Solution:

By using 2nd equation of motion.

                     S = Vit + 1 / 2 at2

                     1000 = 0 x t + 1 / 2 (a)(100)2

                     1000 = 1 / 2 (a) (10000)

                     2000 / 1000 = a

                     0.2ms-2 = a

Now, we can find the final velocity.

                     Vf = Vi + at

                     Using the values.

                     Vf = 0 + (0.2) (100)

                     Vf = 20ms-1

 The speed of the train is 20ms-1

Q#:2.5. A car moves with uniform velocity of 40ms-1 for 5s. It comes to rest in the next 10s with uniform deceleration. Find (i) the deceleration (ii) the total distance travelled by the car?

Ans.Solution:

Given data: 

                                   Part (I)

                             Time = t = 10sec

                             Initial velocity = Vi = 40ms-1

To Find:  

                             Decelration = a = ?

                             Total distance covered = S = ?

Solution:

                              Vf = Vi + at

                             -40 = 10s

                            −40 / 10 = a

                               a = -4ms-2

                                   Part (II)

To find the total distance (s)

                                S = v x t

Using the values, we have.

                                S = 40 x 10

                                S = 400m

The total distance covered by a car is 400m.
